Universal Commerce Protocol overview

How a chat request becomes a checkout

UCP gives AI agents and merchants a shared way to discover products, prepare carts, and hand buyers to a trusted checkout when payment or review is needed.

Presentation-ready summary based on this repository's docs
1

Buyer asks the AI

The request can be plain language: product type, style, budget, size, color, destination, or even a product image.

intent location preferences
2

AI uses the demo connector

This MCP server translates the conversation into UCP-ready product search and checkout requests.

3

Shopify Catalog finds matches

Catalog returns relevant products, images, prices, variants, and shops that can serve the buyer's destination.

products variants availability
4

Cart or checkout is prepared

Once the buyer chooses, the merchant-side UCP endpoint prepares the cart, totals, address fields, and next checkout status.

cart buyer info status
5

Buyer finishes with trust

If payment or review is required, UCP returns a continue URL and the buyer completes the purchase in Shopify-hosted checkout.

continue URL payment order
C

Catalog answers “what should I buy?”

The AI can search across Shopify-powered merchants, narrow by destination, and show product details without sending the buyer to multiple storefronts.

→

Checkout answers “where do I safely pay?”

The merchant remains in control of checkout rules. The agent helps prefill and explain, then hands off when a trusted payment page is the right next step.

One-line takeaway: UCP lets the AI assist the shopping journey while the merchant keeps product truth, checkout policy, and payment trust.
